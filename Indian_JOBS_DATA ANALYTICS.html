CH 02 Geography — Where India Works
Geography isn't just a location filter — it's a salary multiplier. The data reveals that Bengaluru alone commands a 13.7L average (vs 8.7L national), driven by the concentration of high-paying tech and data roles. Delhi NCR cities consistently outperform the national average, while coastal metros follow close behind.
City-by-City: Job Volume vs Average Salary
Blue bars show total job listings (left axis). The orange line tracks average salary (right axis). Notice how job count and salary are not always correlated — Delhi earns more than Bengaluru per role despite fewer listings.

Bengaluru paradox: Despite hosting 21% of all jobs, its average salary (₹8.5L) is below Delhi (₹9.4L) and Gurgaon (₹9.1L) — because it hosts a wider spread of roles at all levels, diluting the average.

CH 07 Analyst Conclusions — The 6 Things That Matter
🏙️
Bengaluru is Volume, Delhi is Value
Bengaluru hosts 21% of all jobs but its average salary (₹8.5L) is lower than Delhi (₹9.4L). For premium compensation, NCR cities outperform despite lower volume.
🧠
Data Science is a 2× Premium
Data Scientists earn 2.1× more than Data Analysts (₹16.4L vs ₹7.9L) — the largest salary gap between two adjacent data roles in the entire dataset.
📊
Excel Dominates but Python Pays
Excel appears in 36% of all listings but Python holders earn ₹1.33L more on average. Excel is ubiquitous; Python is lucrative.
⭐
Seniority is Worth ₹1.73L
The "Senior" prefix consistently adds ₹1.73L to average salary. Job category matters more (39.7% importance) but seniority is the single easiest lever an employee can pull.
🚀
ML Engineers are the Next Scarcity Play
Only 75 ML Engineer listings exist today — the smallest category. But at 35% projected demand CAGR, this will be the most sought-after role by 2027 relative to supply.
🏢
Global Firms Pay the "India Premium"
Adobe, PayPal, Uber, and Visa pay 30–46% above the national average — a clear "global employer premium" that dwarfs even the best domestic company offers.
